About Me

My name is Samuel Vergara, but you can call me Sam. I am a software developer, an artist, a CAD modeler, and more. I studied Mechatronics Engineering at McMaster University and graduated in May 2018. On my spare time I enjoy designing, programming, building, assembling, and more. I like to apply the knowledge learned at McMaster and outside in creative ways, hopefully creating something exciting along the way.

One of the applications I chose to approach was programmatic art generation. This allowed me to create art through programming means and it allowed me to expand on topics I have covered in school. My current works are created with java as well as the processing java library. See Graphics for more info.

Aside from software and art, I also enjoy working with robotics and embedded systems. As an example, I worked alongside a team to develop a robot that would help automate the roofing industry. The robot's purpose was to re-shingle roofs with asphalt shingles.

See RoboRoof for more info on this project.

Recently I have started working on a tabletop wire stripper that cuts wires at a desired length, with the ends already stripped for use in DIY electronics projects.

That is all about me so far. If there's anything else you are curious about feel free to comment and ask! I would love to answer more about myself!
